3. C : The mother of all modern programming languages

The First Inventor of C

C is one of the most influential programming languages ever created. It was developed in the early 1970s by Dennis Ritchie.

1. Dennis Ritchie is the creator of C.
2. Born on 9 September 1941 in Bronxville, New York.
3. Developed C at Bell Labs.
4. C evolved from the B programming language.
5. Used to develop the UNIX operating system.
6. First appeared in 1972.
7. Known as the “mother of modern programming languages”.
8. Influenced C++, Java, C#, Python, and many more.
9. Awarded the Turing Award (1983).
10. Remembered as a giant in computer science.

Ken Thompson – The Co-Creator of UNIX

Ken Thompson

1. Worked at Bell Labs with Ritchie.
2. Co-creator of UNIX operating system.
3. Helped inspire the creation of C.
4. Designed the B programming language.
5. Won the Turing Award (1983) with Ritchie.
6. Advocate of simplicity in design.
7. His work shaped modern computing.
8. Also co-creator of the Go programming language (Google).
9. Known for genius in systems programming.
10. One of the greatest minds in CS history.

Brian Kernighan – The Teacher Who Popularized C

Brian Kernighan

1. Co-author of The C Programming Language (K&R).
2. Worked with Dennis Ritchie.
3. Helped document and teach C.
4. His book became the “C bible”.
5. Contributed to UNIX development.
6. Popularized the use of C globally.
7. Coined the term “Hello, World!”.
8. Influenced generations of programmers.
9. Still active as a computer science educator.
10. Celebrated for his clarity and teaching.

"C is quirky, flawed, and an enormous success." – Dennis Ritchie
